TL;DR

Convexity is the second-order sensitivity of a bond's price to yield changes — it corrects the linear approximation that duration alone gives you.

What it actually is

Duration tells you roughly how much a bond's price moves when yields move 1%. But that relationship isn't actually linear — it curves. Convexity measures that curvature. For small yield changes, duration is close enough. For larger moves, you need the convexity correction to avoid mispricing.

Jargon decoder

Duration — weighted average time to receive a bond's cash flows; proxy for rate sensitivity
Positive convexity — price rises faster than duration predicts when yields fall; a good thing for the holder

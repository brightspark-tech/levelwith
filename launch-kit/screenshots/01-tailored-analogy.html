TL;DR

A Kalman filter is a way to track a moving thing when your measurements are noisy — it blends predictions with new data to give you the best guess.

An analogy for you

Think of it like restoring an old table. You run your hand across the surface to find dips, but your fingers aren't perfect — sometimes they register a blemish that isn't there. A Kalman filter is the part of your brain that says "I know this piece of walnut, I expect it to be smooth here — I'll trust that prediction more than this one sketchy touch." It combines what you expect with what you just measured, weighted by how much you trust each one.

How you might use it

  • You could build a tiny IoT setup in the shop that tracks the humidity of a board over time — the Kalman filter would smooth out the sensor jitter.
  • You could apply the same idea to your GPS watch on trail runs where the signal drops under tree cover.
